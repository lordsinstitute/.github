# Lords Institute of Engineering & Technology

Welcome to the official GitHub Organization of **Lords Institute of Engineering & Technology (LIET)**. This space is dedicated to innovation, collaboration, and practical learning through real‑world software development projects.


## About Lords Institute

Lords Institute of Engineering & Technology is committed to providing industry‑relevant education, encouraging research, and empowering students to build meaningful technological solutions. Through our academic programs, skill academies, and student‑led communities, we nurture creativity, leadership, and professional excellence.

This GitHub organization serves as a central hub for:

* Student projects and mini‑projects
* Workshop and training repositories
* Research and academic work
* Open‑source contributions
* Event resources and hackathon projects


## What You Will Find Here

Our repositories are organized into multiple categories to help students, faculty, and collaborators easily explore our work.

### Academic Projects

Course‑related assignments, mini‑projects, and capstone work from departments including IT, CSE, AI & ML, and Data Science.

### Skill Academy & Workshops

Content from **Lords Skill Academy (LSA)** workshops, including Python, Git, Web Development, AI/ML, and industry‑relevant skill programs.

### Community Projects

Projects created through student communities such as **HackUnion**, CodeWave Hub, and other LIET tech initiatives. These repositories encourage collaboration and open‑source contribution among students across colleges.

### Research & Innovation

Faculty‑student collaborative research, prototypes, experimental tools, and publications.


## Our Mission

To create a collaborative environment where discipline and fun coexist, inspiring students to build impactful technology, learn by doing, and contribute to society through innovation.


## Contributing

We welcome contributions from students, alumni, faculty, and collaborators.

You can contribute by:

* Improving documentation
* Fixing bugs or adding features
* Sharing workshop materials
* Creating open‑source projects
* Participating in hackathons and community events

Please check the contribution guidelines inside each repository before submitting a pull request.
